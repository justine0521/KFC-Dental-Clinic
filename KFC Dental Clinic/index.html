Services We Offer

GENERAL DENTISTRY

provides a wide spectrum of services, including check-ups, cleanings, and treatments, to ensure oral health and address common dental issues for patients of all ages

1. Oral Prophylaxis / Cleaning

Oral prophylaxis is both a preventive measure and a treatment procedure. It involves the complete cleaning of teeth, in order to remove all the remains from foods, plaque, tartar, dead cells, and more.

2. Tooth Restoration / Pasta

When the damage of the teeth reaches an irreparable state, Tooth Filling or what is commonly known as Dental Pasta is performed to restore proper function and structure. It is a procedure which removes the decayed part of the tooth and fills it with a dental restorative material.

3. Tooth Extraction / Bunot

Tooth extraction, or "pag-bunot ng ipin" is the removal of a tooth from its socket in the bone. If you are facing a tooth extraction, it can seem a little daunting and nerve-wracking.

4. Oral Rehabilitation

The treatments can include onlays, crowns, bridges, veneers, dental implants, and/or dentures that will essentially provide not only a "smile makeover", but improved chewing efficiency for the patient. In some patients, other specialties will orthodontics may also be employed to facilitate the best possible outcome.

ENDODONTICS

focuses on diagnosing and treating issues within the tooth's pulp, often through root canal therapy, to preserve natural teeth and alleviate pain or infection.

1. Root Canal Therapy

Root canal treatment is a treatment sequence for the infected pulp of a tooth which is intended to result in the elimination of infection and the protection of the decontaminated tooth from future microbial invasion.

PROSTHODONTICS

dedicated to designing, creating, and fitting dental prosthetics, such as dentures and implants, to restore and enhance oral function and aesthetics.

1. Complete Dentures

A complete denture is a removable appliance used when all teeth within a jaw have been lost and need to be prosthetically replaced. In contrast to a partial denture, a complete denture is constructed when there are no more teeth left in an arch, hence it is an exclusively tissue-supported prosthesis.

2. Partial Dentures

A removable partial denture is a denture for a partially edentulous patient who desires to have replacement teeth for functional or aesthetic reasons and who cannot have a bridge for any reason, such as a lack of required teeth to serve as support for a bridge or financial limitations.

3. Jacket Crowns

In dentistry, a crown or a dental cap is a type of dental restoration that completely caps or encircles a tooth or dental implant. A crown may be needed when a large dental cavity threatens the health of a tooth. A crown is typically bonded to the tooth by dental cement.

4. Fixed Bridge

A fixed bridge, which is sometimes referred to as a traditional dental bridge or a fixed partial denture, is not removable. Rather, it has crowns that are permanently attached to the teeth adjacent to the empty space in your mouth (the abutment teeth).

ORTHODONTICS

addressing misalignments and irregularities of teeth and jaws, using devices like braces to improve function and enhance the aesthetic appearance of the smile.

1. Braces (Metal, Ceramic, Self-ligating)

Dental braces are devices used in orthodontics that align and straighten teeth and help position them with regard to a person's bite, while also aiming to improve dental health.

2. Retainers

Orthodontic retainers are custom-made devices, usually made of wires or clear plastic, that hold teeth in position after surgery or any method of realigning teeth.

3. Mouth Guard

A mouthguard is a protective device for the mouth that covers the teeth and gums to prevent and reduce injury to the teeth, arches, lips and gums. An effective mouthguard is like a crash helmet for teeth and jaws.

PEDIATRIC DENTISTRY

providing specialized dental care to children, emphasizing preventive measures and age-appropriate treatments to promote lifelong oral health habits.

1. Oral Prophylaxis

Dental antibiotic prophylaxis is the administration of antibiotics to a dental patient for prevention of harmful consequences of bacteremia, that may be caused by invasion of the oral flora into an injured gingival or peri-apical vessel during dental treatment.

2. Fluoride Application

Fluoride varnish is a highly concentrated form of fluoride which is applied to the tooth's surface, by a dentist, dental hygienist or other health care professional, as a type of topical fluoride therapy.

3. Pit and Fissure Sealant

A sealant is a clear or opaque plastic material that is applied to the pits and fissures of teeth where decay occurs most often. The purpose of the sealant is to provide a physical barrier to occlude pits and fissures and to protect them from bacteria and food.

4. Extraction

>A tooth extraction is a procedure to remove a tooth from the gum socket. It is usually done by a general dentist, an oral surgeon, or a periodontist. The appearance of normal teeth varies, especially the molars.

5. Space Maintainer

A space maintainer is a dental device used in pediatric dentistry. It helps children avoid the negative side effects of premature loss of their baby teeth.

6. Stainless Steel Crowns

Stainless steel crowns (SSCs) are metal crowns made of stainless steel, nickel and chrome. They are used to restore back teeth (molars) that are decayed, broken down and / or which never formed correctly in the first place.

7. Strip Off Crowns

Strip crowns consist of transparent plastic-like shells that are filled with composite material and fitted over damaged teeth. After the material is cured, the plastic shell is removed.

8. Oral Rehabilitation

Usually, dentists think of an oral rehabilitation as meaning restoration of all of the teeth in a given mouth. However, when only the defective teeth in any mouth are restored, that too could be defined as an oral rehabilitation.

AESTHETIC DENTISTRY

improves smiles with cosmetic procedures like whitening, veneers, and contouring, enhancing aesthetics and boosting self-confidence.

1. Teeth Whitening

Tooth whitening or tooth bleaching is the process of lightening the color of human teeth. Whitening is often desirable when teeth become yellowed over time for a number of reasons, and can be achieved by changing the intrinsic or extrinsic color of the tooth enamel.

2. Veneers

In dentistry, a veneer is a layer of material placed over a tooth. Veneers can improve the aesthetics and function of a smile and protect the tooth's surface from damage. There are two main types of material used to fabricate a veneer: composite and dental porcelain.

3. Stain Removal

Stain removal is the process of removing a mark or spot left by one substance on a specific surface like a fabric. A solvent or detergent is generally used to conduct stain removal and many of these are available over the counter.

MINOR ORAL SURGERY

simple surgical procedures within the oral cavity, often performed in a dental office, addressing issues like tooth extractions or small tissue interventions.

1. Impacted Tooth Removal

Impacted wisdom teeth that are causing pain or other dental problems are usually surgically removed (extracted). Extraction of a wisdom tooth is usually required for: Infection or gum disease (periodontal disease) involving the wisdom teeth.

2. Gum Contouring

Gum contouring removes excess tissue and reshapes your gum line. Dentists use this procedure to treat uneven or “gummy smiles” (when you have extra gum tissue that covers parts of your teeth).

3. Frenectomy

A frenectomy is the removal of a frenulum, a small fold of tissue that prevents an organ in the body from moving too far. It can refer to frenula in several places on the human body. It is related to frenuloplasty, a surgical alteration in a frenulum.

DIAGNOSTIC SURGERY

procedures aimed at obtaining tissue samples or visual insights to identify or confirm a medical condition through surgical intervention for accurate diagnosis.

1. Digital Periapical

A periapical X-ray shows your entire tooth, from the crown to the root tip. This type of X-ray helps your dentist detect decay, gum disease, bone loss and any other abnormalities of your tooth or surrounding bone.

2. Panoramic X-rays

A panoramic radiograph is a panoramic scanning dental X-ray of the upper and lower jaw. It shows a two-dimensional view of a half-circle from ear to ear. This service only available on OPD Dental.

FAQs

  • How can I schedule an appointment?

    To schedule an appointment, you can call our clinic during regular business hours or use our online appointment scheduling system on our website. We'll work with you to find a convenient time for your visit.

  • What should I do if I have a toothache?

    If you're experiencing a toothache, contact our clinic immediately. We'll prioritize your case and schedule an appointment to address the issue. In the meantime, you can take over-the-counter pain relievers and avoid applying heat or cold directly to the affected area.

  • Do you offer orthodontic services for adults?

    Yes, we provide orthodontic services for patients of all ages, including adults. Our orthodontic treatments include traditional braces as well as more discreet options like clear aligners.

  • What should I do if I have a dental phobia?

    If you have dental phobia, please inform our staff when scheduling your appointment. We can discuss strategies to make you more comfortable, such as using sedation dentistry techniques or taking breaks during longer procedures. Open communication helps us create a positive experience for all our patients.

  • How often should I have a dental check-up?

    We recommend regular dental check-ups every six months. These appointments allow us to monitor your oral health, detect any issues early on, and provide preventive care. However, some patients with specific dental conditions may require more frequent visits, as determined by our dental professionals.
